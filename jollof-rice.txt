Ingredients:
 - 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
 - 1/4 cup vegetable oil
 - 1 large onion, finely chopped
 - 1 red bell pepper, seeded and roughly chopped
 - 1 (6-oz) can tomato paste
 - 2 large tomatoes, chopped (or 1 (14-oz) can diced tomatoes)
 - 2–3 Scotch bonnet or habanero peppers (adjust to heat preference)
 - 2 cloves garlic, minced
 - 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced
 - 1 teaspoon thyme (dried or fresh)
 - 1 bay leaf
 - 1 teaspoon curry powder
 - 1 teaspoon paprika
 - 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock
 - 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
 - ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
 - 1 cup mixed peas and carrots (frozen or fresh)

Directions (condensed):
 1. Blend & sauté: Puree bell pepper, tomatoes, Scotch bonnet, garlic, and ginger. In a pot, sauté onion in oil until soft, then stir in tomato paste and spices for 2 min.
 2. Cook sauce: Add your puree; simmer 8–10 min until thickened.
 3. Toast rice: Stir in rice to coat, cook 2–3 min.
 4. Simmer: Pour in stock, season, bring to boil, cover and cook 20 min.
 5. Add veggies: Top with peas & carrots, cook 5–8 min more.
 6. Finish: Discard bay leaf, fluff with a fork, and serve hot.
